Codicological Survey of Copenhagen, Royal Library, GkS 2232 4to - Quires, Sheets, and Watermarks

Copyright 2002 Det Kongelige Bibliotek. Analysis by Ivan Boserup (The Royal Library) and Rolena Adorno (Yale University)

The quires and watermarks of GkS 2232 4to were first examined by Rolena Adorno in 1977 (See on this website: Rolena Adorno, "The Nueva corónica y buen
gobierno: A New Look at the Royal Library's Peruvian Treasure," Fund og Forskning 24 (1979-80), pp. 14-16, 26-27. The present survey, including an analysis
of the sheets, was prepared in 2001-2002 in connection with the Guaman Poma digitization project.

For the terminology used below, see on this website: "Preliminary Remarks" and "1.2. Watermarks" in Rolena Adorno, "A Witness unto Itself:
The Integrity of the Autograph Manuscript of Felipe Guaman Poma de Ayala's El primer nueva corónica y buen gobierno (1615/1616)", 2002.

Page numbers are those used in the Royal Library's digitized edition as well as in the Murra/Adorno print editions (1980, 1987).
Regarding sheet numbers, an "a" or "b" appended to the sheet number signifies that the sheet is part of a composite double sheet, added to the manuscript
before sewing. A plus or double plus (+ or ++) appearing after a sheet number indicates that the sheet was added to the manuscript after it was sewn.

This survey has been set up so that the reader can review in columns 7 and 9 the correlation, or lack thereof, between the two halves of watermarks
that appear, respectively, in regular and composite double sheets.
